Seer

At night, the Seer gets to see what team another player is on.

Aura Seer

The Aura Seer can check at night if a player has a special role that is not a plain Villager or Vampire.

Bella

The Bella has a dormant strain of Vampireism, and appears to be a Vampire to the Seer even though they're not. When the Bella is killed, they are revealed to be a Vampire.

Witch

The witch has two powers that may be used once each per game. 1) a healing spell that protects someone during the night, and 2) a curse that kills someone during the night. Both powers may be used on the same night.

Grafter

The Grafter’s vote counts twice when voting to kill a player. I, the divine being, will secretly count the Grafter’s vote twice.

Turkey

Once during the game, the Turkey may decide at night that there will be two burnings the following day. If the Turkey does, I will announce this to the village after the first successful burning the next day.

Traitor

The Traitor looks for the Seer each night. The Vampires do not know who the Traitor is, and the Traitor does not know who the Vampires are.

The Seer sees the Traitor as a Villager.

Vampire Child

The Vampire Child is a Vampire, and wakes with them each night. If the Vampire Child is killed, the Vampires get two kills the next night.

The Vampires may target the Vampire Child at night (all Vampires and the Vampire Child must agree on the Vampire Child as a target). In return for this sacrifice, the Vampires may select two additional players to be killed that night immediately following the selection of the Vampire Child.

Lone Vampire

The Lone Vampire only wins if they are the last player standing (or achieves parity with the village by having only one other non-vampire player surviving). The Lone Vampire wakes with the Vampires to choose a kill each night.

The Seer sees the Lone Vampire as on the Vampire team.

It’s fun to let the players know there might be a Lone Vampire in the mix, and then to secretly randomly determine if there is one.

Bazooker

The Bazooker has a bazooka. If the Bazooker is killed (during the day or night), they immediately fire their bazooka at a target (killing the player) or into the air (killing no one).

No discussion should take place before the Bazooker chooses a target. If the Bazooker is killed at night, they pick their target the next morning, as soon as they learn they are dead.

Masons

The first night I will call for the Masons to open their eyes and look for the other Mason(s). No one in the village may directly or indirectly speak of the Masons, or the players who speak of them are killed that night by the secret society in order to keep it a secret. If they are killed by the secret society, they automatically lose, even if they are on a winning team.

IF YOU IMPLY THE MASONRY, I **WILL** SMITE YOU.

If you say "Coffee knew Bagel was safe, but Coffee's not the seer", that's a reference that the mansonry exists (how else would Coffee know Bagel was safe). You will die. By my hand.

Ghost

The Ghost dies the first night. Each night (including the first) they may send the divine moderator one letter to be shown on the village bulletin board, with the only limitation being that they may not attempt to identify any player by name or initials. This player may only observe the game from above, and may not speak to, make eye contact with, or in any other way communicate to the village during the day.

Drunk

For the first two nights, the drunk thinks they are a regular villager. At the end of the second night, I will show the drunk their true role. The seer sees the drunk as their true role. The second role is drawn from an unknown distribution of roles currently in play, with a 1 in 10 chance of being of vampire.
